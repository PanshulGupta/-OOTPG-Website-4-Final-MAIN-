The East India company was a British company founded around the 1600’s, by two entrepreneurs, named John Watts, and Thomas Smythe. The East India company was known as one of the most powerful corporation, that was most famous for trading, and the production of goods, along with this this was due to the low salaries of individuals, and the savings were compensated for the increasing prices of import, and export. Around the 1600’s, Queen Elizabeth I had been proposed with an idea of ‘royal charter’, which meant that there were over 70000 pounds invested for trading outside of Europe. The East India company was known to have traded from the Cape of Good hope (Southern tip of Africa), to East Asia (~China). On August 24, 1608, the East India company attempted to sail across to India, to trade Spices, Textiles, etc. at the port of Surat. Gradually, since trade flourished across Southeast Asia, the East India company gradually took control over various states in India, and expanded in the long run. Eventhough the East India company had a privileged position in the British government, it grew significantly due to trading spices during the period of Imperialism, which meant that the company gained rising political, and economic control of India. In 1857, the East India company was defunct, this was because increased corruption, since they treated natives like slaves, and various other events, also including the Indian Stock Market. After the East India company, which controlled India for over 200 years, was declared defunct by the British government, the British empire took control of India.
